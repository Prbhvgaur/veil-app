# Veil

Veil is a premium, privacy-first anonymous messaging concept designed to feel soft, magnetic, and intentionally different from a typical anonymous inbox.

It combines a polished landing page, a simulated sign up and sign in flow, an inbox, chats, requests, a creative studio, and a detailed settings area into one single-file frontend prototype.

## What It Is

Veil is built around the idea that anonymity does not have to feel cold or chaotic. The product presents anonymous messaging as something calmer, more expressive, and more consent-driven.

Instead of a generic chat app, Veil feels like a private space with a brand identity of its own:

- dark, atmospheric visual design
- soft luxury-inspired surfaces
- expressive typography
- privacy-first language
- Gen Z-friendly personality

## How It Works

The prototype is a self-contained HTML experience:

1. Open `veil-app.html` in a desktop browser.
2. Use the landing page to move into sign up or sign in.
3. The auth flow generates verification steps in the UI for presentation purposes.
4. After entry, the app opens into the inbox experience.
5. From there, you can switch between Discover, Chats, Requests, Inbox, About, Studio, and Settings.

The flow is intentionally smooth and visual so it can be reviewed easily without any setup.

## Core Features

- Landing page with premium hero treatment and brand storytelling
- Sign up and sign in screens with a simulated OTP flow
- Inbox view with messages, reactions, and privacy controls
- Chats view with thread switching and tone controls
- Requests view for consent-based connection handling
- Discover view with curated profile cards
- Studio view for prompt, tone, and accent customization
- Settings view for notifications, privacy, appearance, and account controls
- Light and dark theme support
- Mobile-responsive layout and animated UI details

## What Makes It Unique

Veil is different because it treats anonymous messaging like a designed emotional experience, not just a utility.

- Consent is part of the product language, not a hidden setting.
- The interface feels premium and expressive instead of plain or corporate.
- The visual style is intentionally soft, moody, and memorable.
- The product balances safety, mystery, and warmth in one system.
- The UI is structured so it can be shown to recruiters as a concept product, not just a mockup.
